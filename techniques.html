The Techniques

Now that we've covered the theoretical foundations, let's move on to the practical application. This section will demonstrate proven techniques used by the world's top performers.

The Pomodoro technique

25 minutes of focus mode (learning or working on a task) and 5 minutes off (diffuse mode). The time of interval can vary. This technique is great for killing procrastination. It's best to start with the least desiered task.

Chunk the subject

Each letter in the alphabet is its own entity: A,B,C ... but when we make one chunk out of those enities it becomes an entity of its own. For example, adding the letters "c" "a" and "t" creates a word with a meaning. When we encounter the word "CAT" we don't interprit it each time by reading each letter at a time. We've already chunked these letters into the word "cat" and stored it in our long term memory. A way to chunk a subject is to divide and conquer. When we encounter a big problem, we need to break things down to small peices that we can store in our long term memory" was corrected to "A way to chunk a subject is to divide and conquer. Deliberate Practice

When is my focus time? Have I done this before? What's + 1? How caN I gain a plus one point from yesterday? Will I get immediate feedback? Write it down. At the end of the activity or at the end of the day, write down what you've accomplished.

Create a Roadmap

Before we start learning a new topic, we should make a roadmap. A clear patch toward the goal. Research books, posts, reviews, what's relevent and what isn't. recognize quality resources. Use coggle/word doc/google sheet. What topic is the most importanat?
